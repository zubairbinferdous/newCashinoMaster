Cookies

We use Cookie

We use cookies to provide you with a complete variety of services when you visit our website, identify your preferences, and make using our website more comfortable and easy.

What are Cookies?

When you visit a website, a cookie, pixel, or other similar technology saves a tiny file or information on your computer or device. Over time, it allows the website to remember your activities and choices (such as login, language, font size, and other display options). It allows you not to re-enter them each time you visit or go from one page to the next.

Options

You consent to the usage and storing of cookies on your computer or device by using our website.

You may browse our website without cookies in most cases. However, certain features may not function properly, or navigation may be slower.

If you don’t want cookies to be kept on your computer or device, you may disable the appropriate option in your browser's system settings. You may also erase stored cookies from your browser's system settings at any time.

At https://www.aboutcookies.org/cookie-faq/, you can learn how to disable or erase cookies. Please bear in mind. However, refusing to accept cookies may limit the usefulness of our services.

Cookie classifications

Cookies are used for a variety of reasons and functionalities. Some examples of cookies are:

  • required in technical terms (technical necessity);
  • kept and used for a set amount of time (storage duration);
  • placed and saved by us or a third party (cookie provider).

Technical necessity

Technically, cookies are:

  • Necessary. Some of them are used because they are required for the website and its functionalities to function correctly. Unless you have enabled your browser to refuse cookies, these cookies are automatically installed on your PC or device when you go to the website or a specific function.
  • Non-necessary. These are set on your PC or device to enhance the ease and functionality of our website or to save specific preferences that you have made. We also employ cookies that are not necessarily essential in technical terms to collect information on the frequency of usage of particular parts of our website, allowing us to tailor our services to your specific needs.

Time spent in storage

Session cookies. They are only required for the length of your website session. They will be deleted or rendered invalid once you leave our site or your current session runs out. Session cookies, for instance, are used to keep track of specific data during your session.

Permanent cookies. They are kept for a more extended period of time. For example, it enables us to recognize you when you return to our website and access stored preferences. Consequently, you may access web pages more quickly or with better ease, such as without having to re-enter specific information, such as your preferred language. Permanent cookies are removed after a predetermined time period.

Flow cookies. These are used for internal server communication inside our corporate group. They are installed on your computer or device when you begin surfing the website and are erased after completing your navigation. Flow cookies get a unique identification number. However, this information does not allow us to make inferences about the actual client or user.

Cookies providers

Provider cookies. These are put by the operator of our website on our behalf or us.

Third-party cookies. They are saved and used by other organizations or websites, such as Web analytics tools. External providers, such as social plugins, may also employ cookies to show ads or incorporate material from social networks.
